Slow Cooker Dump Meals: 7 Recipes Where You Just Dump and Go

📅 2026-05-25 ⏱️ 3 min read 📝 733 words

Dump meals are exactly what they sound like: you dump everything into a slow cooker in the morning, go to work, and come home to a fully cooked dinner. No browning, no sautéing, no pre-cooking. Just dump and go.

These 7 recipes are family-tested, budget-friendly, and genuinely delicious. Each serves 4-6 people and costs under $15 total.

1. Chicken Taco Soup

Dump these in:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can corn, drained
  • 1 can diced tomatoes with green chiles (Rotel)
  • 1 can tomato sauce
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1 cup chicken broth

Cook: Low 6-8 hours or High 3-4 hours. Shred chicken with two forks before serving.

Serve with: Tortilla chips, shredded cheese, sour cream, avocado, fresh cilantro

Total cost: ~$10 | Per serving: ~$2

2. Mississippi Pot Roast

This viral recipe took the internet by storm — and for good reason. Five ingredients, zero effort, incredible flavor.

Dump these in:

  • 3-4 lb chuck roast
  • 1 packet ranch dressing mix
  • 1 packet au jus gravy mix
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 5-6 pepperoncini peppers (from a jar)

Cook: Low 8 hours. The roast will fall apart when you touch it with a fork.

Serve with: Mashed potatoes, egg noodles, or on hoagie rolls as sandwiches

Total cost: ~$14 | Per serving: ~$3

💡 Why It Works: The ranch and au jus packets create a savory, tangy sauce. The pepperoncini add a subtle vinegary heat. And the butter makes everything rich. It sounds weird, but trust the process.

3. Honey Garlic Chicken

Dump these in:

  • 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • ½ cup honey
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil

Cook: Low 4-5 hours or High 2-3 hours.

Serve with: Rice and steamed broccoli. Spoon the sauce from the slow cooker over everything.

Total cost: ~$8 | Per serving: ~$1.60

4. Italian Sausage and Peppers

Dump these in:

  • 1.5 lbs Italian sausage links (sweet or hot)
  • 3 bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper

Cook: Low 6-7 hours or High 3-4 hours.

Serve: On hoagie rolls with provolone cheese, over pasta, or with crusty bread

Total cost: ~$11 | Per serving: ~$2.20

5. White Chicken Chili

Dump these in:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cans Great Northern beans (or cannellini), drained
  • 1 can corn, drained
  • 1 can diced green chiles
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • 4 oz cream cheese (add in last 30 minutes)

Cook: Low 6-8 hours. Shred chicken, stir in cream cheese during last 30 minutes until melted and creamy.

Serve with: Tortilla chips, sour cream, shredded Monterey Jack, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime

Total cost: ~$11 | Per serving: ~$2.20

6. Beef and Broccoli

Dump these in:

  • 1.5 lbs beef chuck or sirloin, sliced thin
  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated

Cook: Low 4-5 hours. Add 3 cups broccoli florets in the last 30 minutes. Mix 2 tablespoons cornstarch with 3 tablespoons water and stir in to thicken the sauce.

Serve over: Steamed rice. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.

Total cost: ~$12 | Per serving: ~$2.40

7. Loaded Baked Potato Soup

Dump these in:

  • 6 large russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper

Cook: Low 6-8 hours until potatoes are very tender. Mash some potatoes in the pot for thickness (leave some chunky). Stir in 1 cup heavy cream and 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese.

Top with: Crispy bacon bits, sour cream, chives, extra cheese

Total cost: ~$9 | Per serving: ~$1.80

🎯 Key Takeaway: Slow cooker dump meals are the ultimate weeknight dinner hack. Each recipe costs $8-14 total (under $3 per serving), requires zero cooking skill, and gives you a hot, homemade dinner waiting when you walk in the door. Make one of these every week and you'll save $50+ per month compared to takeout.
