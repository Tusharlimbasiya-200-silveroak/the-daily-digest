Best Free Software Everyone Should Have on Their Computer in 2026

📅 2026-05-25 ⏱️ 3 min read 📝 514 words

You don't need to spend hundreds on software. In 2026, there are free programs that match or beat their paid competitors. Here are the essential free tools everyone should have on their computer.

Productivity

  • LibreOffice — Free replacement for Microsoft Office. Writer (Word), Calc (Excel), Impress (PowerPoint). Opens and saves in Microsoft formats. Perfect for home use.
  • Notion — All-in-one workspace for notes, to-do lists, databases, and project management. Free for personal use. Replaces Evernote, Trello, and Google Keep.
  • Obsidian — Powerful note-taking app that stores everything as plain text files on your computer. Your notes, your data, forever.

Security

  • Bitwarden — Free password manager that generates and stores strong, unique passwords for every account. Cross-platform sync between phone and computer.
  • Windows Defender — Already built into Windows 11. It's genuinely good now — you don't need to buy antivirus software anymore.
  • Proton VPN — Free VPN with no data limits. Slower than paid VPNs but fully functional and trustworthy.
💡 Pro Tip: Stop paying for antivirus software. Windows Defender is free, built-in, and consistently ranks among the top antivirus solutions in independent tests. Norton, McAfee, and Avast are unnecessary bloat in 2026.

Creativity

  • GIMP — Free alternative to Photoshop. Steep learning curve but incredibly powerful for photo editing and graphic design.
  • DaVinci Resolve — Free video editor used by actual Hollywood studios. It's not a watered-down version — the free tier includes professional color grading and visual effects.
  • Audacity — Free audio editor for podcasts, music editing, and voice recording. Simple interface, powerful features.
  • Canva — Free graphic design tool for social media posts, presentations, and flyers. Drag-and-drop interface that anyone can use.

Utilities

  • 7-Zip — Opens any compressed file format (ZIP, RAR, 7Z). Small, fast, and completely free. Never pay for WinRAR.
  • VLC Media Player — Plays every video and audio format ever created. No codec packs needed. It just works.
  • ShareX — Screenshot and screen recording tool that does everything. Capture, annotate, upload, share. Far more powerful than the built-in Snipping Tool.
📌 Real-Life Example: Freelancer Amy was spending $350/year on Adobe Creative Suite, Microsoft Office, and a VPN. She switched to DaVinci Resolve, LibreOffice, and Proton VPN. "The free tools do everything I need. I saved $350/year and the only thing I miss is Adobe's cloud storage."

Communication

  • Signal — End-to-end encrypted messaging. More private than WhatsApp, simpler than Telegram.
  • Thunderbird — Free email client from Mozilla (the Firefox people). Handles multiple email accounts beautifully.
  • Zoom — Free tier allows 40-minute meetings. For personal use, that's usually plenty.
🎯 Key Takeaway: The must-install free software in 2026: Bitwarden (passwords), VLC (media), LibreOffice (documents), and Proton VPN (privacy). These four alone replace hundreds of dollars in paid software. DaVinci Resolve for video and GIMP for photos are genuinely professional tools that happen to be free.
