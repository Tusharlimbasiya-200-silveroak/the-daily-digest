10 Best Road Trip Routes in America: Scenic Drives You Must Take

📅 2026-05-25 ⏱️ 4 min read 📝 801 words

There's something about a road trip that flying can't replicate. The freedom to stop whenever you want, the changing scenery outside your window, the random roadside diners that become your favorite memories. America was built for road trips — here are the 10 best routes to experience it.

1. Pacific Coast Highway (California)

Distance: 655 miles | Time: 3-5 days | Best Season: May-October

The PCH from San Francisco to San Diego is the most iconic drive in America. You'll pass through Big Sur (jaw-dropping cliffs above the ocean), Monterey (sea otters and world-class aquarium), Santa Barbara (wine country vibes), and Malibu (celebrity sightings and perfect beaches).

The stretch through Big Sur alone — where the mountains plunge straight into the Pacific — will be the most beautiful road you've ever driven.

2. Blue Ridge Parkway (Virginia to North Carolina)

Distance: 469 miles | Time: 2-4 days | Best Season: October (fall colors)

Known as "America's Favorite Drive," the Blue Ridge Parkway winds through the Appalachian Mountains with overlooks that'll make you pull over every 10 minutes. In October, the fall foliage turns the mountains into a canvas of reds, oranges, and golds that photography can't capture.

💡 Pro Tip: The speed limit on the Blue Ridge Parkway is 45 MPH — and that's intentional. This isn't a drive to rush through. Budget more days than you think you need, stop at every overlook, and eat at roadside BBQ joints.

3. Route 66 (Chicago to Santa Monica)

Distance: 2,448 miles | Time: 7-14 days | Best Season: Spring or Fall

The Mother Road crosses 8 states and 3 time zones. It's a drive through American history — quirky roadside attractions, neon-signed motels, diners that haven't changed since the 1950s, and landscapes that shift from Midwest flatlands to painted deserts.

4. Going-to-the-Sun Road (Montana)

Distance: 50 miles | Time: 1 day | Best Season: July-September (only months it's fully open)

This narrow, winding road through Glacier National Park is the most dramatic short drive in America. You'll climb to Logan Pass at 6,646 feet with glaciers, waterfalls, and mountain goats visible from your car window.

📌 Real-Life Example: College friends Jake and Tom drove Route 66 over spring break. "We spent $1,200 each for 10 days — gas, motels, and food. We saw the Grand Canyon at sunset, ate at a diner in Texas that served the best pie of our lives, and stayed at a wigwam motel in Arizona. It was the trip of a lifetime."

5. Overseas Highway (Florida Keys)

Distance: 113 miles | Time: 1 day | Best Season: December-April

Driving from Miami to Key West across 42 bridges over crystal-clear turquoise water is surreal. The Seven Mile Bridge section — where you're surrounded by nothing but ocean on both sides — is unlike anywhere else on earth.

6. Kancamagus Highway (New Hampshire)

Distance: 34.5 miles | Time: Half day | Best Season: October

The "Kanc" is the best fall foliage drive in New England. Short enough for a half-day trip, stunning enough to be unforgettable. Stop at Sabbaday Falls for a quick waterfall hike.

7. Columbia River Gorge (Oregon/Washington)

Distance: 80 miles | Time: 1-2 days | Best Season: April-June

Massive waterfalls (Multnomah Falls is 620 feet tall), lush green forests, and the mighty Columbia River. This drive is Pacific Northwest beauty distilled into 80 miles.

8. Hana Highway (Hawaii)

Distance: 64 miles | Time: Full day | Best Season: Year-round

620 curves and 59 bridges through Maui's tropical rainforest. Black sand beaches, bamboo forests, and waterfalls at every turn. It's the most unique driving experience in the US.

9. Beartooth Highway (Montana/Wyoming)

Distance: 68 miles | Time: Half day | Best Season: June-September

Charles Kuralt called it "the most beautiful drive in America." You'll climb to 10,947 feet through alpine meadows and snow-capped peaks. The gateway to Yellowstone's northeast entrance.

10. Natchez Trace Parkway (Tennessee to Mississippi)

Distance: 444 miles | Time: 2-3 days | Best Season: Spring (wildflowers) or Fall

A hidden gem that most travelers overlook. No commercial traffic allowed, no billboards, just pure natural beauty and historical sites along a route used by Native Americans for centuries.

🎯 Key Takeaway: Start with the Pacific Coast Highway if you've never done a major road trip — it's accessible, iconic, and consistently breathtaking. For fall, the Blue Ridge Parkway is unbeatable. For adventure, Going-to-the-Sun Road in Montana is a bucket-list drive. The beauty of road trips is that the journey IS the destination. Budget more time than you think you need — the best moments are the unplanned stops.
