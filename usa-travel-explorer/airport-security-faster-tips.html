How to Get Through Airport Security Faster: Insider Tips That Work

📅 2026-05-25 ⏱️ 2 min read 📝 526 words

The airport security line is the most stressful part of any trip. The shuffling, the shoe removal, the laptop juggling, the person in front of you who apparently has never flown before. But with the right preparation, you can cut your wait time by 50-70% and start your trip relaxed instead of frazzled.

1. Get TSA PreCheck ($78 for 5 Years)

This is the #1 best travel investment. TSA PreCheck gives you a dedicated shorter line where you:

  • Keep your shoes ON
  • Keep your laptop IN your bag
  • Keep your belt and light jacket ON
  • Keep liquids IN your bag

Average wait: 5 minutes or less (vs 15-30+ for regular lines). Apply at tsa.gov, do a quick in-person appointment, and get approved in 1-2 weeks.

2. Wear the Right Clothes

  • Slip-on shoes (no laces to untie)
  • No belt (or wear one you can remove in 2 seconds)
  • No metal jewelry or accessories
  • Pockets completely empty before you get in line
💡 Pro Tip: Put your phone, wallet, keys, and watch into your bag BEFORE getting in the security line. Not when you reach the bins. This saves 30-60 seconds and eliminates the panic of leaving something behind in a bin.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

The ideal time depends on your destination and preferences. Generally, shoulder seasons (spring and fall) offer the best combination of good weather, smaller crowds, and lower prices. Avoid major holiday weekends for better deals.

A comfortable daily budget in the US ranges from $100-200 per person for mid-range travel. This includes accommodation ($80-150/night), food ($30-50/day), and activities ($20-50/day). Budget travelers can manage on $50-80/day.

Pack layers regardless of season, comfortable walking shoes, a reusable water bottle, phone charger, and any necessary medications. Check weather forecasts a week before your trip. Roll clothes to save space and always pack a rain jacket.

For trips under 300 miles, driving is usually cheaper, especially for groups. For longer distances, compare flight prices (book 3-6 weeks ahead for best deals) vs. gas costs. Factor in rental car costs and parking fees at your destination.

For popular destinations and national parks, reservations are highly recommended, especially during summer and holidays. Book accommodations 2-3 months ahead and restaurant reservations 2-4 weeks ahead for popular spots.
