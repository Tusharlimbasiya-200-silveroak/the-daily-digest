How to Drink More Water: Simple Tricks That Actually Work

📅 2026-05-25 ⏱️ 3 min read 📝 649 words

You know you should drink more water. Everyone knows. But knowing and doing are different things. If your water bottle has been sitting untouched on your desk for the past 3 hours, these tricks will help you drink more without thinking about it.

How Much Water Do You Actually Need?

The "8 glasses a day" rule is a myth. Your actual need depends on your body size, activity level, and climate. A simple formula: drink half your body weight (in pounds) in ounces of water. So if you weigh 160 lbs, aim for 80 oz (about 10 cups).

But the easiest indicator? Check your urine. Pale yellow = hydrated. Dark yellow = drink more. Clear = you might be overdoing it.

Trick 1: Keep Water Visible

If your water bottle is in the kitchen and you're in the living room, you won't get up to drink. Keep a water bottle within arm's reach at all times — on your desk, on the couch, by your bed. Out of sight = out of mind. In sight = you'll sip without thinking.

Trick 2: Drink a Full Glass First Thing in the Morning

Before coffee, before breakfast, before checking your phone — drink a full glass of water. You wake up dehydrated after 7-8 hours without water. This single habit gives you a head start every day.

💡 Pro Tip: Put a glass of water on your nightstand before bed. When your alarm goes off, the first thing you see is water. Drink it before your feet hit the floor. It becomes automatic within a week.

Trick 3: Add Flavor

Plain water is boring for some people, and that's okay. Add natural flavor:

  • Lemon or lime slices
  • Cucumber and mint
  • Frozen berries (they act as ice cubes and flavor the water as they thaw)
  • A splash of juice (not the whole glass — just a splash for taste)

Trick 4: Use a Bottle You Like

This sounds silly, but it works. If you like your water bottle — the way it looks, how it feels, how the cap works — you'll carry it everywhere and drink more. Invest $20-30 in a bottle you genuinely enjoy using.

Trick 5: Eat Water-Rich Foods

About 20% of your daily water intake comes from food. Eat more:

  • Watermelon (92% water)
  • Cucumbers (96% water)
  • Oranges (87% water)
  • Strawberries (91% water)
  • Lettuce (96% water)
📌 Real-Life Example: Nurse Patricia used to drink maybe 3 cups of water a day despite working 12-hour shifts. She bought a 32-oz bottle with time markers and kept it at her station. "Having it right there, with marks showing where I should be by noon, changed everything. Now I drink 80+ oz daily and my headaches are gone."

Trick 6: Link Water to Existing Habits

Drink a glass of water:

  • Every time you get up from your desk
  • Before every meal
  • Every time you go to the bathroom
  • During every commercial break (if watching TV)
  • Every time you check your phone

By linking water to things you already do, drinking becomes automatic.

What Counts Toward Your Water Intake?

  • Yes: Water, herbal tea, sparkling water, milk, fruits
  • Partially: Coffee and regular tea (they're mildly diuretic but still hydrating overall)
  • No: Alcohol, sugary sodas (these actually dehydrate you)
🎯 Key Takeaway: Two changes will double your water intake: keep water visible at all times and drink a full glass every morning before anything else. Don't try to force 8 glasses all at once — build the habit gradually by linking water to things you already do. Your body will thank you with more energy, fewer headaches, better skin, and better digestion.
